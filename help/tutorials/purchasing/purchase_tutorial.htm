Purchasing Tutorial

Activity required : purchasing.rw
Plug-in required : Purchasing, Project Management, Project Purchase

Incremental Epic handles purchasing from two perspectives. It fully integrates the purchasing process into project management, as well as offering a complete, standalone purchasing control system.

Note however what Epic is not. Incremental Epic does not offer stores nor asset management functionality, nor is it an accounting system.

A yard may choose to retain its existing purchasing system and use Epic's extensive customisation capabilities to integrate with this existing system. Or it may choose to use Epic as its primary purchasing system.

This tutorial will look at both options. It will start with the dedicated purchasing functionality, and then look at how this is fully integrated into project management.

This tutorial covers:

  • purchase item
  • purchasing screen
  • purchase item wizard
  • purchase item editor
  • project management integration
  • purchase item alerts

Purchase Item

The core 'unit' in purchasing is a purchase item. This is either a tangible material or a bought-in service. Purchase Items have a lifeline just as other core concepts in Incremental Epic such a projects and tasks. A purchaser creates a purchase item and then progresses through its lifeline until it is used by production. Purchasing Screen

The purchase plug-in gives access to the dedicated purchase screens. Switch to the 'Purchases' plug-in. Initially the top left of the screen holds all the functionality:

  1. View purchase items
  2. View suppliers (if added)
  3. Create a new purchase item
  4. Change status of selected purchase item
  5. Refresh the visible list
  6. Filter visible purchase items by status

The purchase items at a particular status are displayed. Use the 'filter' dropdown to select which status to look at.

Note that the supplier tab may or may not have a full list of suppliers in it. This will depend on whether Epic is your primary purchasing system. If it is not there may be no need to store all the suppliers within Epic.

Purchase Item Wizard

Create a new purchase by clicking the '+' icon. This opens the purchase wizard. Note that only three bits of information are required by Epic: the description, whether materials or subcontract, and the cost center. This is in keeping with Epic's approach of requiring as little information as possible. You can add much more - but you are not forced to.

The cost center is what the purchase cost is assigned to. It is normally a task in an open project. Both task and project must be at specific states. The wizard automatically gives you a list of applicable cost centers:

Epic can be configured with various different rules for who can authorise purchase items. If the user has permission, they will be offered the option to immediately authorise the purchase item. Else it will be given the status of 'Requested'.

When the wizard closes, you can choose to automatically open the purchase request editor. Alternatively you can double click on the purchase item on the purchase screen lists.

Purchase Item Editor

The purchase item editor, like all editors has a read-only and a read-write mode. In read-write mode you can see all the possible information that can be recorded for a purchase item. It is up to the yard to decide what information Epic manages.

Summary

Core information about the purchase item. The 'Accounts Code' is yard-specific and can be made mandatory, or simply ignored.

Alerts

A means for purchasing and production departments to communicate - see purchase item alerts later.

Payment Details

Most information here only necessary if Epic is the primary purchase systems. However the cost is important for invoice generation so may be subject to other controls such as task alerts.

Lifeline

A graphical representation of the lifeline. Clicking on a status will show dates and durations. Right-clicking will allow you to change the purchase item's status.

Delivery / Arrival

A means to track deliveries.
Actions A history of the purchase item - who did what, when.

Locations

 (Only visible once delivery made).
Once the purchase item arrives, its location can be tracked. Moreover epic can be configured to enforce a QA process such as all arrived materials going via a quarantine area.
Notes Arbitrary text notes can be added about the purchase item.
Documents Later versions of Epic (1.5.8+) permits documents to be stored against purchase items in the document repository.

The editor also allows for the purchase item to be 'exported', ie. sent to another program such as Microsoft Word or Excel. Such exports are yard-specific and are how hard copies of purchase orders, receipts etc. can be created.

A purchaser then processes the purchase, adding information as required, and reports progress by changing the status and moving the purchase item along its lifeline. This is done either by the button at the top right of the editor or by right-clicking the lifeline. Project Management Integration

Those parts of the purchasing process that a project manager is involved with are integrated directly into the project management screens. Switch to the 'Project Management' plug-in and open the project to which your new purchase was assigned. The last tab on the project editor lists all purchases for the project:

The list can be sorted by any of the columns and full information on a purchase item is immediately to hand by double-clicking, which opens the purchase item editor.

Clicking on the 'tasks' tab and selecting an individual task will show all the purchases from that task, as well as summary cost information. Again double-clicking will open the full purchase item editor.

From here the project manager can also initiate a purchase request. Clicking the 'request new service/subcontract' or 'request new materials' button will open the purchase item wizard, and the new purchase item will immediately appear on the screen of the purchasers.

Purchase Item Alerts

A core inefficiency in many yards is communication between production and purchasing departments with one side waiting on the other for information of one sort or another. Epic offers purchase item alerts as one mechanism for mitigating this issue.

Modeled on task alerts, purchase item alerts present a red/yellow/green 'traffic light' icon for each purchase item. If someone needs information from another they can raise an alert:

This will affect the traffic light icon. All lists of purchase items can be sorted by these icons so that users can focus on those items that are highlighted as needing attention.
